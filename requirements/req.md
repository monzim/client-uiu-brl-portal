Welcome to the Biomedical Research Laboratory
The Biomedical Research Laboratory of the Department of Pharmacy at United International University (UIU) is dedicated to advancing scientific knowledge in the field of pharmaceutical and biomedical sciences. Our laboratory serves as a dynamic platform for innovative research, academic excellence, and collaborative learning.
We focus on addressing critical healthcare challenges through experimental research, modern analytical techniques, and interdisciplinary approaches. Our work bridges the gap between basic science and clinical applications, aiming to improve human health and therapeutic outcomes.

3. Research Areas
Cell Culture
Cell culture research involves the growth and maintenance of cells under controlled laboratory conditions. This area is essential for studying cellular behavior, drug responses, toxicity, and disease mechanisms. Our laboratory utilizes cell culture techniques to evaluate the effectiveness and safety of pharmaceutical formulations, including hydrogels and nanoparticles.

Wound Healing
Wound healing research focuses on developing and evaluating materials and therapies that enhance tissue repair and regeneration. Our work includes studying hydrogel-based systems, antimicrobial dressings, and bioactive compounds that accelerate healing, reduce infection, and improve tissue recovery.


Gene Polymorphism
Gene polymorphism research examines variations in DNA sequences and their impact on disease susceptibility, drug response, and therapeutic outcomes. This area helps in understanding personalized medicine and identifying genetic factors involved in various diseases.
Antimicrobial Resistance
Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) is a growing global health concern. Our research focuses on identifying resistant microbial strains and evaluating new antimicrobial agents, natural extracts, and advanced formulations to combat resistant infections effectively.

List of Current running projects:
•	Hydrogel Project Title and description

•	Colistin Resistance and mcr-1/mcr-3 Gene Analysis in Urinary E. coli from a Tertiary Level Hospital 
This project investigates the emergence of colistin-resistant Escherichia coli isolated from urinary tract infections in a tertiary-level hospital setting. Special emphasis is placed on detecting plasmid-mediated resistance genes mcr-1 and mcr-3, which play a critical role in the global spread of antimicrobial resistance. By combining microbiological culture techniques, antibiotic susceptibility testing, and molecular analysis, the study aims to identify resistance patterns and genetic determinants. The findings will contribute to improved surveillance, infection control strategies, and rational antibiotic use to combat multidrug-resistant pathogens in clinical settings.

•	Role of VEGF Polymorphisms and Serum VEGF Level as Potential Biomarkers in Breast Cancer: A Case-Control Study in Bangladeshi Population 
This project explores the relationship between genetic variations in the Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor (VEGF) gene and circulating serum VEGF levels as potential biomarkers for breast cancer in the Bangladeshi population. Using a case–control study design, the research aims to identify associations between VEGF polymorphisms, disease susceptibility, and progression. By integrating molecular genetics with clinical data, the study seeks to improve early detection, risk assessment, and prognostic evaluation of breast cancer, ultimately contributing to more personalized and effective healthcare strategies.


•	In vitro and In vivo Biological Activities and In Silico Analysis of Diospyros malabarica Leaf Extract 
This project investigates the therapeutic potential of Diospyros malabarica leaf extract through a comprehensive approach combining in vitro, in vivo, and in silico analyses. The study evaluates its biological activities, including antioxidant, antimicrobial, and pharmacological effects, using laboratory and animal models. Additionally, computational methods such as molecular docking are employed to identify potential bioactive compounds and their mechanisms of action. The findings aim to support the development of plant-based therapeutic agents and provide scientific validation for traditional medicinal uses.
